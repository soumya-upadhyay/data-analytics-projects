# 🛒 E-Commerce Sales & Customer Behavior Analysis (SQL Case Study)

This project analyzes a simulated **e-commerce dataset** to uncover insights about customer behavior, product performance, sales trends, and inventory dynamics.

The case study was completed as part of my **SQL practice and portfolio development** to demonstrate how SQL can be used to answer real business questions in an e-commerce environment.

---

# 🔍 Business Problem Statements

The objective of this analysis was to answer several real-world business questions using SQL.

### Customer Insights
1. Which **cities have the highest number of customers**?
2. How does **customer engagement vary based on order frequency**?
3. What type of customers dominate the platform (one-time buyers, occasional shoppers, regular buyers)?
4. What are the **monthly trends in new customer acquisition**?

### Product Performance
5. Which **products generate high revenue with consistent purchase quantities**?
6. Which **product categories attract the largest number of customers**?
7. Which products are purchased by **less than 40% of customers**?

### Sales Trends
8. How does **monthly sales revenue change over time**?
9. Which month experienced the **largest decline in sales**?
10. How does the **average order value fluctuate month-to-month**?
11. Which months represent the **peak sales periods**?

### Inventory & Operations
12. Which products have the **highest turnover rates** and require frequent restocking?
13. Which months require **higher inventory levels and staffing**?

# 🧠 Analytical Approach

## 1️⃣ Data Exploration
First, I examined the structure of the dataset by reviewing the following tables:

- Customers
- Products
- Orders
- OrderDetails

Understanding table relationships helped structure joins and aggregations later in the analysis.

---

## 2️⃣ Customer Segmentation

SQL queries were used to analyze:

- Geographic distribution of customers
- Order frequency patterns
- Customer acquisition trends

This helps businesses understand **who their customers are and how engaged they are with the platform**.

---

## 3️⃣ Product & Category Analysis

Using joins across **Products, Orders, and OrderDetails**, I identified:

- High-performing products
- Categories attracting the most customers
- Low engagement products purchased by fewer than 40% of customers

This analysis highlights **inventory and marketing opportunities**.

---

## 4️⃣ Sales Trend Analysis

To understand sales performance, I analyzed:

- Month-on-month revenue trends
- Percentage change in sales
- Average order value fluctuations
- Peak sales periods

Window functions like **LAG()** were used to measure month-to-month changes.

---

## 5️⃣ Inventory Insights

Finally, I examined product turnover rates to determine:

- High-demand products
- Items requiring frequent restocking
- Months where inventory demand peaks

---

# 📊 Key Insights

Some key insights from the analysis include:

- **Delhi, Chennai, and Jaipur** have the highest customer concentration.
- **Occasional shoppers** make up the largest portion of the customer base.
- **Electronics** is the product category with the highest customer reach.
- Sales trends **fluctuate significantly rather than steadily increasing**.
- **February 2024 experienced the largest decline in sales.**
- **Product ID 7 has the highest turnover rate**, indicating strong demand.
- Some products are purchased by **less than 40% of customers**, suggesting low visibility or limited demand.

---

# 📌 Strategic Recommendations

Based on the findings:

- Focus marketing campaigns on **high customer concentration cities**.
- Use **loyalty programs or personalized offers** to convert occasional shoppers into repeat customers.
- Improve **visibility of underperforming products** through targeted promotions.
- Increase inventory levels during **September and December peak sales months**.
- Monitor sales fluctuations to adjust **pricing and promotional strategies**.
